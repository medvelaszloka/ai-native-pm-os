## The Shape Up Cycle

### Cycle Structure
- **6 weeks:** Building time
- **1 week:** Cool-down (bug fixes, exploration, small improvements)
- **Planning:** During the build cycle
- **Betting:** Last week of the build cycle

### Key Phases
1. **Framing** - Define the problems to work on
2. **Shaping** - Define bets (your role!)
3. **Betting** - Leadership selects which bets to fund
4. **Building** - Teams execute for 6 weeks
5. **Cool-down** - Breathing room, no major commitments

---

## How Bets Are Defined

### What Makes a Good Bet
A bet is shaped when it has:
- Clear problem statement
- Rough solution approach
- Appetite (time boundary)
- What's in/out of scope
- Evidence of demand
- Feasibility assessment

### Bet Appetites
- **Small Bet (2 weeks):** Small scope, clear path
- **Big Bet (6 weeks):** Full cycle, more complex

### Bet Pitch Format

## 1. Problem
_What problem are we trying to solve? Why does it matter? What are the proof points?_

## 2. Appetite
_How much capacity are we willing to spend on this problem in terms of weeks?_

## 3. Measuring Success
Main KPI and other indicators.

## 4. Solution
_What are the core elements of the solution, key features and workflows?_

## 5. Rabbit Holes
_What potential complexities or time sinks should we avoid?_

## 6. No-Gos
_What are we explicitly not doing? What's out of scope?_

## 7. Fat Marker Sketches & Designs
_High level flows and visual concepts showing the core elements_

## 8. Definition of Done
_Checklist for delivery_

## Common Bet Patterns

### Types of Bets
- **Customer-driven:** Direct customer requests with clear demand
- **Strategic:** Aligns with company direction/goals
- **Technical:** Infrastructure, performance, technical debt
- **Exploratory:** New territory, higher uncertainty
- **Efficiency:** Internal tools, workflow improvements

### Red Flags (Bets That Usually Don't Get Selected)
- Too vague on problem
- No clear evidence
- Not aligned with strategy
- Missing scope definition

## Terminology

**Appetite:** How much capacity (in weeks) we want to spend (not an estimate)
**Pitch:** The formal bet proposal document
**Shaping:** Defining the bet before committing to build
**Scope Hammer:** Cutting scope to fit appetite
